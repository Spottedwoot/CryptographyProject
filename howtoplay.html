How To Play and the Math Behind Encryption

How To Play

The encryption game has 2 main portions: encryption and decryption. The purpose of the game is to show you why encryption works by displaying the reason why it is hard to break an encryption, but not hard if you know the key. Modern encryption uses the fact that it is significantly easier to take the product of two primes then to find the 2 unique prime factors of a large number. So in the encryption portion of the game you will be given 2 primes and need to find (p-1)(q-1), which is the value that is used to decrypt text. For the decryption you will be given a number that factors into two prime numbers p and q and will need to find (p-1)(q-1). You will find that the decryption is significantly harder than the encryption. In the sandbox you will be able to type in whatever text you want and encrypt it and then take the encrypted text and decrypt it. Have fun!

How Encryption Works

The way encryption works is it starts with two people. Let's call them Alice and Bob. Alice and Bob want to communicate and so they both choose 2 large primes each pA and qA for Alice and pB and qB for Bob and keep them secret. Then they will both choose a number eA for Alice, and eB for Bob. Then Alice will publish NA=pAqA and eA while Bob will publish NB=pBqB and eB. Now let's say Alice wants to send a message to Bob. Alice will first convert her message to Bob to a number, m. She will then compute the remainder of meB when divided by NB. This may seem like a daunting task given that calculating powers of a number is hard but in practice there are many tricks to calculate this remainder fast so that when a computer does it, it becomes about as hard as calculating mk where k is about log2(eB). This means that even for relatively big eB calculating this remainder doesn't get too bad. Now Alice will send that remainder, c, to Bob. Then Bob will find a d such that when he divides d*eB by (pB-1)(qB-1) he gets a remainder of 1. This while also looking hard to solve, is not very difficult for a computer to do. Finally Bob will compute the remainder of cd when divided by NB and that will get him back the original m. This fact is not obvious and takes some effort to prove but that is outside of the scope of this game.

Like regular equations you can add, subtract, and multiply on both sides, but be careful about division because divsion only works if the number you are dividing by and n don't share any factors. This is why taking the remainder of meB divided by NB is not that bad because you only have to care about the remainder after each step of the computation. As you have read about previously, in the real world congruences are mostly just used in cryptography.
